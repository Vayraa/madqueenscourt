  1. Light VehicleÂ armor. On an APC, combat helicopter, or equivalent. You can’t wear this, unless it’s on an exoskeleton or something.
  2. Heavy VehicleÂ armor. Actual tank plating. You can’t wear this, ever.

Personal armorÂ is damaged before your HP, except by critical hits, weapons that say they penetrate its level of protection or better, and stuff that would ignore armor logically (falling damage, etc).

The important parts of a firearm are its **Size, Ammo,Â **Action/Fire Modes,Â and Recoil.

**SizeÂ **is pretty obvious. Can you fire it effectively with one hand or not. How many slots does it take up in your inventory. That sort of thing.

Actionsdetermine what fire modesÂ are available with a firearm and if it can be used for iterative attacks:

  • Single-shot firearms have to be reloaded between each attack. Break-action shotguns and rifles are probably the most common form of this, but there are some single-shot bolt-action rifles as well.
  • Manual (pump, lever, bolt, single, etc)-action firearms have to be manually cycled between each attack. It’s assumed that you’re doing this automatically as long as you have a free hand, but it means you can only make one attack with them per round even if you have multiple attacks.
  • Semi-automatic firearms fire one shot with each pull of the trigger. You can use them to make as many attacks as you have in a round. If you’re a Fighter,Â maybe you can also double tapÂ to fire a 2-roundÂ burstÂ with one.
  • Fully automaticÂ firearms fire continually while the trigger is held. You can probably only fire burstsÂ and full-autoÂ with them. If you’re a Fighter,Â maybe you can also use them to fire single shots.
  • Select-fireÂ firearms can be used as if they were either semi-Â or fully automatic.

  • BurstÂ fire costs 3 ammo,Â doublesÂ recoil, and adds 1 die of damage. A double-barreledÂ firearm counts as firing a burstÂ if you fire both barrels.
  • Full-autoÂ fire costs 5Â ammo, treblesÂ recoil, and either adds 2Â diceÂ of damage orÂ hits everyone in a cone (roll the attack roll once, compare to all ACs or have them roll Dex vs your attack or whatever).

  • -4 recoilÂ if set up on a bipod or fired from a prone position, etc.
